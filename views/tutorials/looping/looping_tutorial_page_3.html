Why

Sometimes, you will want to repeat a loop based on something other than just counting.
A "while" loop handles these sorts of things.

What

Again, two different types of while loops, though they can be used the same ways.
  • The first is the standard 'while' loop.
    It needs a comparison, but will run the loop over and over until the comparison is 'false'.
    Make sure you put a way for it to be false, or you'll run into an infinite loop quite easily!

  • The second is a bit weird. It's an 'until' loop.
    It's kind of an inverse of the 'while' loop.
    It works the same way though, repeating a loop based on a comparison it checks.
    The difference being that it goes until the comparison is true!
    The keen-eyed among you may realize that you could set up a while loop to do the same thing.
    That is very true, but using the 'until' loop makes things a bit easier by seperating it.

How

Drag out the block to the workspace.
Decide and pick which kind of loop you want, 'while' or 'until'.
Drag on and slot in the comparison you want, and put in the variables you want to compare.
Finally, just fill in the body of the loop and you are good to go!
