Why

This lets you begin moving into more complicated stuff.

If you're walking around and like the look of some flowers, you might want to take some home.

Well, you probably don't want to take all of them, since it means they won't be there if you want more later.

What

This remains pretty straight-forward.
Two branches means you want an "if" and an "else" part to your block.
That way, you set up what happens once you find the flower you want.
But, as you pick them, you won't keep taking them as until there's none left.


How

To do this, you'll want to click on the little cogwheel in the top left of the "if" block.
Then, drag the "else" block onto the main "if" block, just like how you connect blocks normally.
Then, in the main workspace, connect in whatever other blocks you want to use.
